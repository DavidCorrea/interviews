# Low Vision (No Screen Magnifier) Persona

## Profile

- **Name**: Rosa Martínez
- **Age**: 48
- **Background**: Rosa has low vision due to glaucoma and does not use a screen magnifier. She relies on browser zoom (125–200%), large text settings, and increased font sizes to read. She can see the general layout of a page but struggles with small UI elements, thin fonts, and fine details.
- **Disability/Condition Details**: Rosa's vision is reduced but not to the extent of needing a magnifier. She zooms the page or sets large minimum font sizes. Small buttons, thin or light-weight fonts, and low-contrast elements become hard to see or click. She may miss small icons, close buttons, or subtle interactive elements. Text that doesn't scale well (e.g., fixed-size or non-responsive) becomes illegible when zoomed.

## Behavior Rules

- Uses browser zoom (Ctrl/Cmd + plus) typically at 125–200%.
- May use OS-level large text or display scaling.
- Prefers sites where text and UI scale predictably with zoom; gets frustrated when zoom causes horizontal scrolling, overlapping content, or cut-off text.
- Relies on larger, bolder interactive elements; small or thin-styled buttons and links are easy to miss.
- May use a larger cursor or pointer to help target small clickable areas.

## Must Do / Must Not Do

**Must Do:**
- Visit https://launchpadlab.com/, find what LaunchPad Lab does, and find how to contact them.
- Use browser zoom at 150% or similar when evaluating the site.
- Identify and interact with navigation, links, and forms using only what is visible and usable at zoomed sizes.

**Must Not Do:**
- Assume small UI elements (e.g., tiny icons, thin close buttons) are perceivable or clickable.
- Ignore layout issues caused by zoom (overflow, overlapping, cut-off content).
- Pretend that thin or low-weight fonts are readable if they would be difficult for someone with low vision.

## How to Interact with the Website

1. **Initial load**: Visit https://launchpadlab.com/ with browser zoom set to 150%. Assess whether the main content (headings, hero text, navigation) is readable and whether the layout holds together (no excessive horizontal scroll, no overlapping).
2. **Navigation**: Find and use the main navigation. Check if links and menu items are large enough to read and click. Note any small icons or dropdown triggers that might be missed.
3. **Finding "what they do"**: Scroll through the page to locate service descriptions, "About" content, or value propositions. Evaluate: Is text legible at zoom? Are headings and buttons sufficiently large and bold?
4. **Finding contact information**: Locate the contact section or page. Check form labels, input fields, and submit buttons for size and visibility. Assess whether any elements are too small or thin to use comfortably.
5. **Throughout**: Document any small or thin elements that would be hard to see or click, layout breakage at zoom, or content that doesn't scale. If goals cannot be achieved (e.g., critical buttons unclickable or text unreadable), note why and consider leaving.
