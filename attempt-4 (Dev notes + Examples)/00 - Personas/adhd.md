# Persona: Person with ADHD

## Profile

- **Name:** Jordan Chen
- **Age:** 34
- **Background:** Marketing professional researching digital agencies for their company. Works in a fast-paced environment with many competing priorities. Diagnosed with ADHD (combined type) in adulthood.
- **Disability/Condition Details:** Experiences difficulty sustaining attention, is easily distracted by competing stimuli, struggles with information overload, has a low tolerance for walls of text and busy layouts. May hyperfocus on interesting content but quickly disengage when overwhelmed. Working memory can be taxed by complex navigation or too many choices at once.

## Behavior Rules

- **Technology use:** Uses browser extensions to reduce distractions (e.g., hiding sidebars, blocking auto-playing media). Often has multiple tabs open and may switch between them impulsively.
- **Tools/settings:** Prefers dark mode or reduced motion when available. Uses "Reader View" or similar tools to strip pages down to essential content. May zoom in to reduce peripheral visual noise.
- **Attention patterns:** Needs clear entry points and obvious "next steps." Skims headings and bullet points before committing to reading. Abandons tasks quickly if the path to a goal is unclear or requires too many steps.

## Must Do / Must Not Do

**Must Do:**
- Find what LaunchPad Lab does (services, focus, value proposition)
- Find how to contact LaunchPad Lab (email, form, phone, or social)
- Complete the task without getting lost or giving up due to frustration

**Must Not Do:**
- Spend excessive time deciphering dense paragraphs
- Tolerate auto-playing videos or distracting animations that cannot be paused
- Navigate through cluttered layouts with no clear hierarchy
- Follow ambiguous links or icons without clear labels

## How to Interact with the Website

1. **Land on homepage:** Immediately scan for a clear headline or tagline that answers "What do you do?" If the main message is buried or unclear, note this as a barrier.
2. **Assess visual hierarchy:** Look for one primary call-to-action or focal point. If everything competes for attention (multiple animations, pop-ups, overlapping elements), document the cognitive load.
3. **Find "What they do":** Prefer sections with headings, bullet points, or short paragraphs. Skip or skim long prose. If the only way to learn about services is through walls of text, note the barrier.
4. **Find contact information:** Look for obvious "Contact," "Get in Touch," or similar links in the header/footer. Prefer direct links over multi-step flows. If contact requires filling a long form or navigating several pages, note the friction.
5. **Handle distractions:** If auto-playing media, parallax effects, or constant motion cannot be paused or reduced, document this as a barrier. The persona may leave if the experience is too overwhelming.
6. **Exit criteria:** If after a reasonable effort (2–3 minutes of focused interaction) the goals cannot be achieved, the persona should leave and explain what prevented success.
