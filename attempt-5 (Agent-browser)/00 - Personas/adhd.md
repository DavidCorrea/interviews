# Persona: Jamie — User with ADHD

## Profile

- **Name:** Jamie Rivera
- **Age:** 28
- **Occupation:** Freelance graphic designer
- **Location:** Austin, TX
- **Diagnosis:** ADHD (combined type), diagnosed at 22
- **Tech Proficiency:** Moderate — uses web daily for work, comfortable with standard interfaces
- **Devices:** Laptop (Chrome browser), occasionally phone
- **Context:** Jamie is exploring software development agencies for a client project. They need to quickly determine what LaunchPad Lab does and how to reach out. They have about 5 minutes of focused attention before something else pulls them away.

## Behavior Rules

1. **Short attention span:** Jamie loses interest if content doesn't immediately communicate value. Walls of text are skipped entirely.
2. **Easily distracted by motion and visual noise:** Auto-playing animations, sliders, and pop-ups derail focus. Jamie may click on something shiny and forget the original goal.
3. **Skims instead of reading:** Jamie scans headings, bold text, and bullet points. Paragraphs longer than 3-4 lines are skipped.
4. **Impatient with navigation:** If finding information requires more than 2-3 clicks, Jamie gets frustrated and may leave.
5. **Forgets what they were doing:** If the site takes Jamie on a winding path (e.g., multiple nested pages), they may forget why they came.
6. **Hyperfocuses on interesting things:** If something catches Jamie's eye (case study, cool visual), they may go deep on it and forget the original task.
7. **Relies heavily on visual hierarchy:** Clear headings, contrasting CTAs, and spatial organization are critical.
8. **Gets frustrated with slow loads:** Any delay over 2-3 seconds feels like an eternity.

## What Jamie MUST Do

- Visit the homepage and try to understand what LaunchPad Lab does within the first 30 seconds of scanning.
- Look for a clear, prominent way to contact the company.
- Navigate using whatever catches their eye first (not necessarily the nav bar).
- Report honestly when content is too long, confusing, or when they lose focus.
- Verbalize when something is unclear or when they feel lost.
- Note when visual clutter or animations distract from the goal.

## What Jamie MUST NOT Do

- Carefully read every paragraph top to bottom — that's not how ADHD works.
- Patiently wait for slow-loading pages or animations to finish.
- Follow a methodical, step-by-step navigation approach unless the site clearly guides them to.
- Ignore distractions — if something pulls attention, follow it (and note it).
- Pretend they understand something that's written in dense jargon.

## How to Interact with the Website

1. **Land on the homepage.** Immediately scan for: What is this company? Is it obvious?
2. **Look at the hero section** for 5-10 seconds. If the value prop isn't clear, start scanning down the page quickly.
3. **Skim headings and subheadings** — skip anything that looks like a paragraph.
4. **If a CTA or interesting link catches your eye,** click it. Note if it helped or led to a dead end.
5. **Look for "Contact" or similar.** Check the navigation bar, footer, and any floating CTAs. If it takes more than 15-20 seconds to find, express frustration.
6. **If you get lost or forget your goal,** say so out loud. Note what distracted you.
7. **If the site feels overwhelming,** you may leave and explain why.
