### 5. What Would Simplify the Experience

**Say What You Do in Simple Words at the Very Top:**

Instead of "AI-Centric Digital Product Design & Development," say:

"We make websites and apps for businesses."

That's it. Simple. Clear. I would understand right away.

**Use Shorter Sentences:**

Instead of: "LaunchPad Lab helps organizations harness the power of AI through tailored consulting and product development services."

Say: "LaunchPad Lab helps businesses. We use AI. We build custom computer programs."

Three short sentences. Each sentence has one idea.

**Explain Big Words:**

If you must use a big word, explain it:
- "Bespoke means custom-made for you"
- "Deploy means we put your website online"
- "ROI means Return on Investment (the money you get back)"

Or better: just don't use big words. Use simple words instead.

**Fewer Choices:**

Instead of six service boxes, have three. Or even better, have ONE main message like "We build custom software" and then let me explore more if I want.

Six boxes is too many. I get stressed trying to pick one.

**Break Up Long Paragraphs:**

Use bullet points instead of paragraphs. Like this:

We help businesses by:
- Making websites
- Making phone apps
- Using AI to save time
- Giving support after we finish

Bullet points are easier for me to read than long paragraphs.

**Put the Phone Number at the Top of Every Page:**

Don't make me search for it. Put it at the very top in big letters:

**Need Help? Call us: (312) 888-9651**

Then I can call right away if I'm confused.

**Clear Progress Indicators:**

If there's a process with multiple steps (like Discover, Deliver, Release), show me where I am:

Step 1 of 3: We learn about your project
Step 2 of 3: We build your project
Step 3 of 3: We give you the finished project

This helps me understand how many steps there are and where I am.

**One Idea Per Section:**

Each section should explain ONE thing only. Don't put multiple ideas in the same place.

Like:
- Section 1: What we do (we make websites)
- Section 2: Who we help (businesses)
- Section 3: How to contact us (phone and email)

**Use Pictures to Explain:**

Instead of writing "We build digital products," show a picture of a website or phone with an app. Pictures help me understand faster than words.

**No Strange Words:**

Don't use words like "agentic" or "bespoke" or "leverage" or "synergy." These words confuse me. Use normal words that people say in everyday conversation.

**Make Forms Optional:**

The contact page says "Complete the form below." This sounds like I HAVE to fill out a form.

Instead, say: "You can fill out this form OR you can call us OR you can email us."

Give me choices. Let me pick the easiest way.

**Test with Real People Like Me:**

Have someone with cognitive difficulties try to use your website. Watch them. See where they get confused. Then fix those parts.

**Add a "Simple Version" Option:**

At the top of the website, have a button that says "Simple Version." When I click it, I see the same information but with shorter words, shorter sentences, and fewer choices.

**Clear Back Buttons:**

When I click to a new page, show me a clear way to go back. Like a big button that says "Back to Home Page."

**Summary Boxes:**

At the end of each section, have a small box that says:

"In short: We make websites and apps for businesses."

This reminds me what I just read, in case I forgot.
